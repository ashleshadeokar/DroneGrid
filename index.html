About Us

Adaptable Multispectral UAS With Laser Target Designation

Our research focuses on developing adaptive multispectral image fusion methods optimized for object detection during all hours of the day. By fusing Red-Green-Blue (RGB) and Long-Wave Infrared (LWIR) sensors combined with a modified YOLOv5 model, our method will outperform traditional single-sensor object detection methods. Furthermore, the sensor fusion level will be automatically adjusted based on illumination and ground/air temperatures from the edge device’s attached photosensor and thermometer. Finally, this research will develop a low-cost and open-source laser-target designator for multispectral object detection applications.

The focus of our research is developing Adaptive Multispectral Image Fusion methods that optimize object detection during all hours of the day. By fusing RGB and LWIR sensors, combined with a modified YOLOv5 model, our models will have better detection at higher confidence levels. Furthermore, the fusion level will be automatically adjusted based on illumination and ground temperatures from the edge device’s attached photosensor and thermometer. The codebase will be adaptable to any dual-sensor ISR system, for greater interoperability between low-cost dual-sensor UAS and loitering munitions. Finally, this research develops a low-cost and open-source laser-target designator that estimates geolocation data from object classes detected by an optimized multispectral YOLO model.

Our Research

Research Plan

Fusion Research

Our research explores the use of multispectral sensors, including RGB, NIR, and LWIR, to determine optimal sensor fusion based on temperature and illumination for enhanced object detection.

YOLO Adaptation Based on Optimal Fusion

Designing custom YOLO models that are optimized for the most effective sensor fusion.

Creating Large Multispectral Synthetic Dataset

Generating multispectral datasets with a variety of object classes using transfer learning, LLMs, and auto-segmentation techniques to minimize manual labor.

Developing Low-Cost Precision Geolocation

Adaptive multispectral object detection and object tracking combined with geolocation capabilities.
